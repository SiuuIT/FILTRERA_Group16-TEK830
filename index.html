Aligning FILTRERA with IKEA's principles

Usability:

FILTRERA is built for both managers and on-site team leaders to easily understand and act on safety data. Through intuitive heatmaps and clear summaries, users can identify risk areas without technical training. Ongoing improvements focus on cleaner visual scaling, clearer color coding, and new interactivity features ‚Äî such as clickable zones and severity-based filtering ‚Äî to make day-to-day use even more intuitive.

Feasibility:

FILTRERA is developed in Flutter with a Python-based filtering backend, using IKEA‚Äôs existing safety report data. In the current prototype, the system aggregates incidents by location and severity using synthetic data that mirrors IKEA's existing safety report data. This approach allows safe testing and realistic functionality without handling sensitive company data. The model processes both structured fields and free-text descriptions, ensuring flexibility for future integration with systems such as ServiceNow or Power BI. The goal is a transparent, explainable filtering logic that makes it clear how and why specific insights are generated.

Viability:

FILTRERA directly supports IKEA‚Äôs focus on employee safety and data-driven improvement. By helping teams quickly identify where and why incidents occur, it turns raw data into actionable insights. Future development will include threshold-based alerts and automated summaries to support faster decision-making ‚Äî strengthening the tool‚Äôs role as a decision-support system rather than a passive dashboard.

Sustainability:

FILTRERA promotes social sustainability by making safety data more accessible and actionable, helping prevent workplace incidents and reducing time spent on manual sorting. By processing data locally, the system can maintain privacy and align with IKEA‚Äôs values of trust and responsibility. In the long term, proactive safety features such as pattern-based alerts will support continuous improvement and safer working environments across IKEA‚Äôs operations.
