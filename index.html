FILTRERA provides faster decision-making!

FILTRERA is an intuitive app for safety managers that makes it easy to analyze incident and accident reports. Using smart filters and AI, the app highlights only the most relevant information, saving time and helping you make better decisions

Aligning FILTRERA with IKEA's principles

Usability:

FILTRERA is built for safety managers to easily understand and act on safety data. Through intuitive heatmaps and clear summaries, users can identify risk areas without technical training.

Feasibility:

FILTRERA is developed in Flutter with a Python-based filtering backend, using IKEA‚Äôs existing safety report data. This ensures flexibility with current systems such as kiosks and workstations on-site. The current prototype uses synthetic data that mirrors IKEA's existing safety report data structure. This allows for safe testing without handling sensitive company data. Our goal is a transparent, explainable filtering logic that makes it clear how and why specific insights are generated.

Viability:

FILTRERA directly supports IKEA‚Äôs focus on employee safety and data-driven improvement. By helping teams quickly identify where and why incidents and accidents occur, it turns raw data into actionable insights. Future development will include threshold-based alerts and exporting of automated summaries as PDFs to support faster decision-making ‚Äî strengthening the tool‚Äôs role as a decision-support system rather than a passive dashboard

Sustainability:

FILTRERA promotes social sustainability by making safety data more accessible and actionable, helping prevent workplace incidents and accidents and reducing time spent on manual sorting. By processing data locally, the system can maintain privacy and align with IKEA‚Äôs values of trust and responsibility. In the long term, proactive safety features such as pattern-based alerts will support continuous improvement and safer working environments across IKEA‚Äôs operations

AI Usage

How are we utilizing AI in our project?

Great question! Here are some ways AI is integrated into our project:

üßæ Smart Data Understanding

FILTRERA‚Äôs AI analyzes both structured fields (such as date, location, and type of incident/accident) and unstructured free text from safety reports. It aggregates information by location and severity to highlight patterns and trends

üìä Severity Weighting

Each incident and accidents is scored by severity level from 1 - 10. The heatmap scales its color intensity based on these weights, allowing users to distinguish between frequent but low-impact events and rare but severe ones

üìù Summarization & Insights

AI automatically summarizes multiple incident and accident reports into key points, combining both structured and free-text data. This makes it easier to understand root causes and recurring trends without reading every report individually

üõ†Ô∏è Recommended Actions

The AI summarizes key issues for each area and suggests actions to prevent future incidents and accidents, making it easier for safety manager to take action.

üîî Proactive Alerts (Next Step)

A future development direction is an AI assistant that could notify users when certain patterns rise. For example, a sudden increase in injuries in a specific zone. This supports proactive data-driven safety management
